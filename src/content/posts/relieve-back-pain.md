</span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-bbrsq"><span><span>Poor sitting or standing posture  </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-fmoab"><span><span>Improper lifting, bending or reaching Core stabilization exercises improve posture, balance, strength, power, and coordination, and strengthens the back. Yoga, Pilates and Tai Chi help build a stronger core. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-pg15"><span><strong><span>Move your whole body,</span></strong><span> especially your feet, ankles, knees, hips, back, shoulders, and arms. Move intuitively, however feels good, without forcing a painful stretch.  </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-c24ej"><span><span>Use </span><strong><span>correct posture</span></strong><span> and proper body mechanics in daily activities. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-6jeqp"><span><span>Practice </span><strong><span>stress management</span></strong><span> regularly.  </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-d6dui"><span><span>Eat a</span><strong><span> nutritious diet.</span></strong><span> Include adequate calcium and vitamin D for strong bones. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-dc46q"><span><strong><span>Quit smoking.  </span></strong></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-9vppj"><span><strong><span>Get a 60-90 minute massage every 2 weeks.</span></strong><span> A professional massage therapist may be able to work out the knots. It may also improve posture. When rolling your upper back, stop at the bottom of your rib cage. Avoid rolling your lower back since it is relatively nonmobile and the surrounding muscles could spasm. To release your lower back, try rolling the muscles that connect to it instead, including your glutes, hip flexors, and quads. </span></span></p></li></ul></div><div type="bulleted_list"></div><div></div><div><div id="viewer-7fj05"><div><figure><div><div id="7fj05"><wow-image id="fa29ad_de5c6a43a78445feb6cfd197cda93818~mv2.gif"><img alt="" loading="lazy" src="/images/relieve-back-pain/07_fa29ad-de5c6a43a78445feb6cfd197cda93818-.gif"/></wow-image></div></div></figure></div></div></div><div type="image"></div><div></div><div><ul><li><p id="viewer-24t8h"><span><strong><span>Hang upside down. </span></strong><span>Try different stretching poses, aerial yoga, or an inversion table to stretch the back muscles. Hanging upside down can lengthen the ligaments and ease painful symptoms, says </span><a href="https://www.si.com/edge/2016/09/15/aerial-yoga-health-benefits-hanging-upside-down" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>Dr. Allan Stewart</span></u></span></a><span> of Mount Sinai Hospital in NYC. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-bkt6g"><span><strong><span>Try free hanging. </span></strong><span>Free hanging on monkey bars at the playground or pull-up bars at the gym elongates the vertebrae and improves grip strength. Lay on your back, preferably on a yoga mat, with your legs up a wall. While in this position as close to 90 degrees as possible, push against the very top of your legs, as if you are trying to push your legs away from you. You will feel a release of pressure in your lower back. You could also lock your elbows if this stretch begins to strain your arms. Hold this stretch for as long as comfortable, then relax. Repeat 3-4 times as necessary.</span></span></p></li></ul></div><div type="bulleted_list"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-6v6bq"><span id="sitting-and-driving-6v6bq"></span><span><span>Sitting and Driving </span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><ul><li><p id="viewer-88ba0"><span><span>Maintain your spine in a neutral, relaxed position. Avoid slouching or hunching your body forward. If your chair, seat, or car doesn't provide adequate lower back support, use a lumbar cushion or rolled towel. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-1stru"><span><span>Distribute your body weight evenly on both hips with buttocks touching the back of the chair. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-9ce32"><span><span>Position your feet flat on the floor. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-fu7pm"><span><span>Keep your knees level with or slightly lower than your hips. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-c123f"><span><span>Keep your shoulders back and relaxed and position your head so that ears are aligned over shoulder; avoid thrusting your head forward at the neck. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-bvapr"><span><span>Avoid sitting in bed doing work. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-b21k5"><span><span>Long commutes can exacerbate back pain. To minimize strain, sit at a comfortable distance from the steering wheel. Sitting too far away increases pressure on your lower spine and can stress your neck, shoulders and wrists. Sitting too close can increase your risk of injury from the airbag. Allow 10 inches between the center of the airbag cover and your breastbone. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-kqf4"><span><span>Take breaks; get out, stretch and move around during long trips. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-eugeo"><span><span>Even when maintaining good posture, sitting for long periods at a time can tire the back muscles. Take breaks every hour; stand up, stretch and go for a short walk. </span></span></p></li></ul></div><div type="bulleted_list"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-dpb1m"><span id="standing-dpb1m"></span><span><span>Standing </span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-56nef"><span><span>The most ergonomic work stations involve a combination of sitting, standing, and walking. Adjustable desks and treadmill desks are best-case scenario amenities which few companies offer. </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><ul><li><p id="viewer-b3fli"><span><span>Maintain a straight, neutral spine; avoid slouching sideways or forward. Don't exaggerate the arch in your lower back. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-eg3jg"><span><span>Keep your chin level and head centered over the shoulders. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-ftti"><span><span>Stand with your feet slightly less than shoulder width apart and your knees slightly bent. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-2kpj"><span><span>Lightly contract your abdominal muscles. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-162li"><span><span>Avoid prolonged standing; if not possible, shift your weight from side to side or place one foot on a step or other low footrest. A pillow placed between your knees also helps to reduce back stress. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-8ngtm"><span><strong><span>On your stomach:</span></strong><span> this is not a recommended sleeping position. If you must, keep one leg bent about 90 degrees at the hip and knee and the same side arm bent about 90 degrees at the shoulder and elbow. Hugging a body pillow can make this position easier to maintain. </span></span></p></li></ul></div><div type="bulleted_list"></div><div><p id="viewer-aovlq"><span><span>Regardless of your sleeping position, use a pillow that supports your cervical spine. Choose one that contours to your head and neck while still providing support. </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-3ob3o"><span><span>There are many choices for back-friendly beds (mattresses). The ideal surface for most people is one that is gently supportive and medium-firm to firm and with no sagging. It should mold to your body while supporting it at the same time. </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-401sp"><span id="lifting-401sp"></span><span><span>Lifting </span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-cd2ia"><span><strong><span>Overexertion </span></strong><span>injuries are common in sports with inadequate training, as is often the case with children.</span><strong></strong><span>It takes seconds to obtain a back injury, but the effects can last for years. All people, including children, should be educated on proper lifting form.  </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-6oiq8"><span><span>Yoga, stretching, cardio, and strength training can help strengthen the core (abs, back, and glutes) and reduce the likelihood of overexertion injuries.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-a0m2e"><span><span>When lifting, follow these important steps: </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><ul><li><p id="viewer-7m7lk"><span><span>Lift only what you can handle, and get help if you need it. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-1icp1"><span><span>Ensure safe and firm footing. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-eqnev"><span><span>Stand close to the object to be lifted with feet apart for balance. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-11g0f"><span><span>Squat down to pick up the object by bending at the knees and keeping your back straight. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-8l8ao"><span><span>Secure a firm hold on the object. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-5peq1"><span><span>Tighten your abs and slowly lift using your legs, not your back. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-6pcd9"><span><span>Avoid twisting while lifting. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-5hups"><span><span>Always lift and carry the object close to your body, at about waist level. </span></span></p></li><li><p id="viewer-5dusq"><span><span>Perform the same technique when lowering an object. </span></span></p></li></ul></div><div type="bulleted_list"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-d4pd9"><span id="comfortable-fashion-d4pd9"></span><span><span>Comfortable Fashion </span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-2gbh1"><span><span>Wear flat or low-heeled shoes that offer good support and comfort. If you must, make sure the strap is wide and padded, adjust the strap length so it can go over the head and rest on the opposite shoulder, or switch shoulders often. Evenly distribute the weight, explore rolling bags, or get straps that go across chest/waist. </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-cca62"><span><span>These tips can help with generalized back pain.
