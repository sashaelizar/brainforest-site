<section class="VQDdIN" data-hook="post-description"><div><div><div><div><div><div type="first"></div><div><div><p id="viewer-foo"><span><span>Fast-paced, consumerist cultures have a tendency to value perpetual productivity at the expense of the individual. Although the 40-hour workweek continues to be standard practice, productivity continues to decrease. Being "on all the time" is unrealistic, and excess busiwork can lead to stress, burnout, fogginess, and demoralization. Balance and mindful action are key, and to continue growing we must recharge our energy and protect our spare time.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-114ck"><span><span>Whether you like to get out into nature, read, sleep, sweat, or create, leisure is a powerful healing tool for the body, mind, and spirit. There's nothing like the feeling of getting lost in a parallel literary universe, entering flow through art, music, or dance, or the natural high of a great workout. Our passions give us purpose, elevate our consciousness, and rejuvenate and refocus us.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-a5lho"><span id="why-is-leisure-so-valuable-a5lho"></span><span><span>Why is leisure so valuable?</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><h3 id="viewer-3c0bu"><span id="1-physical-healing-and-regeneration-3c0bu"></span><span><span>1. Physical Healing and Regeneration</span></span></h3></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-3alv3"><span><span>De-stressing supports cell repair and renewal. Unwinding helps you manage </span><a href="https://www.livestrong.com/article/438983-what-are-the-health-benefits-of-leisure-recreation/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>stress</span></u></span></a><span> and lowers depression.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h4 id="viewer-bq5qj"><span id="sleep-bq5qj"></span><span><span>Sleep</span></span></h4></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-b86s5"><span><span>Sleep, a deep relaxation and recovery state, facilitates the </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5103291/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>repair</span></u></span></a><span> of double-stranded breaks in DNA that accumulate during the daytime. When you relax, your </span><a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/ncb0106-9" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>DNA relaxes</span></u></span></a><span>, and that helps it undergo repair. Sleep is </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5390528/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>important</span></u></span></a><span> for so many reasons, including development, energy conservation, clearance of neural waste products, modulation of immune responses, cognition, and performance. Insufficient quality sleep increases the risk of infectious diseases, cardiovascular disease, </span><a href="https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/full/10.1146/annurev-psych-010213-115205" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>cancer</span></u></span></a><span>, depression, and dementia later in life.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h4 id="viewer-8ui87"><span id="movement-and-dance-8ui87"></span><span><span>Movement and dance</span></span></h4></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-4drvb"><span><span>Several studies show that even a single workout session can significantly </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4314337/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>increase BDNF</span></u></span></a><span> (brain-derived neurotrophic factor), which supports neuronal survival and growth. These effects were more prolonged with regular exercise. Another study showed that </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6040685/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>dance</span></u></span></a><span> as opposed to repetitive exercise led to greater increases in brain matter density in more brain regions, including the cingulate cortex, insula, corpus callosum and sensorimotor cortex.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h4 id="viewer-2f868"><span id="meditation-2f868"></span><span><span>Meditation</span></span></h4></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-4uknj"><span><span>It's no secret that meditation is great for you. Meditation has been shown to improve </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19743551" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>brain plasticity</span></u></span></a><span> and memory, increase </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4923750/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>telomerase</span></u></span></a><span> activity, reduce oxidative stress, increase brain </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3004979/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>gray matter</span></u></span></a><span> density, elevate BDNF, and upregulate the </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5483482/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>anti-inflammatory</span></u></span></a><span> cytokine IL-10, strengthening your immune system.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><div id="viewer-606fe"><div><div></div></div></div></div><div type="html"></div><div><h4 id="viewer-95ob8"><span id="natural-living-95ob8"></span><span><span>Natural living</span></span></h4></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-1c9o4"><span><span>Industrialization heralded an era when humans decided to separate themselves from nature, build walls and cities, and become domestic. Despite medical advances that prevent infectious disease and increase lifespans overall, unique circumstances (e.g., excess lifestyles paired with scarcity mindsets, pollutants, and other systemic factors) have increased the incidence of autoimmune diseases in the West.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-1vak0"><span><span>Environmental risk factors, including exposure to lead, mercury, aluminum, cadmium, arsenic, manganese, solvents and some pesticides have been linked to </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4392704/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>neurotoxicity</span></u></span></a><span>, mitochondrial dysfunction, and protein aggregation in AD (Alzheimer's disease) and PD (Parkinson's disease). Additionally, a recent study showed that living close to </span><a href="https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(16)32399-6/fulltext" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>major roads</span></u></span></a><span> increases the risk of neurodegenerative diseases like PD and MS (multiple sclerosis). Taken together, these findings show that human health is maximized by fresh air and maintaining a relationship with nature.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><div id="viewer-246sk"><div><figure><div><div id="246sk"><wow-image id="fa29ad_79f2e653144744ab8926baf7d6c0d900~mv2_d_3840_2160_s_2.jpg"><img alt="" loading="lazy" src="/images/leisure/02_fa29ad-79f2e653144744ab8926baf7d6c0d900-.jpg"/></wow-image></div></div></figure></div></div></div><div type="image"></div><div><h3 id="viewer-846qh"><span id="2-creativity-expands-with-distance-and-time-846qh"></span><span><span>2. Creativity Expands with Distance and Time</span></span></h3></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-3t9ld"><span><span>Humans seek novelty and self-expression in the form of music, dance, writing, and art. Downtime is necessary for creativity. Producing art is therapeutic and can alleviate mental illnesses from anxiety to depression to PTSD. Music can also improve one's abilities in math.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-14mul"><span><span>Leisure is productive, not procrastinatory. Lower stress fosters resourceful thinking. Life is meant to be enjoyed, so do what makes you feel most present, alive, and electrified. Go with your gut, and don't edit yourself while you're in flow.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-adk4l"><span><span>Major ideas may need</span><strong><span> time to incubate</span></strong><span> before they surface. For example, my most productive editing happens after I've forgotten about my writing for a few days and return to it with fresh eyes. I also work much better if I go to sleep and continue studying in the morning, when my head is clear and my senses are reawakened.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-4rm3r"><span><span>Relaxation sends us into a state of elevated consciousness and diffuse focus. Daydreaming or diverting our focus for a period of time leaves space for intuition and ideas to emerge. Counter-intuitively enough, sometimes when we're feeling </span><strong><span>stuck or creatively blocked</span></strong><span>, taking a break could catalyze the creative flow we've been seeking.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><div id="viewer-ab4qd"><div><figure><div><div id="ab4qd"><wow-image id="fa29ad_33d5872df5dc4b21b25e21de7b4f8630~mv2_d_3840_2160_s_2.jpg"><img alt="" loading="lazy" src="/images/leisure/01_fa29ad-33d5872df5dc4b21b25e21de7b4f8630-.jpg"/></wow-image></div></div></figure></div></div></div><div type="image"></div><div><h3 id="viewer-eirhg"><span id="3-spiritual-progress-eirhg"></span><span><span>3. Spiritual Progress</span></span></h3></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-12o2t"><span><span>Making time for yourself is both healing and revitalizing. It seems so simple, but once we get the chance to just be, our essence is revealed. Invest in yourself unapologetically and relentlessly, because every single moment, there you are.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-7ecnb"><span><span>It's amazing how you can go for a walk within your mind, truly seem to mentally wander somewhere else, only to wake up from your daydream with the realization that </span><strong><span>your mind is its own universe</span></strong><span>. This profound breakthrough can catalyze the desire to connect with others, which is also powerful stress relief. Once we take care of ourselves, we are best poised to help others grow and thrive, further fueling the positive feedback loop of love and connection.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><p id="viewer-a5vtb"><span><span>Make time to do what calls you, what makes you happy, and surrender to the flow.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div></div><div type="last"></div></div></div></div></div></div></section>
