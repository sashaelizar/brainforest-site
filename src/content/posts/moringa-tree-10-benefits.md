A deciduous tree, </span><em><span>Moringa oleifera</span></em><span> is commonly grown in partially dry, tropical, and subtropical areas. India is the leading producer, yet </span><em><span>Moringa oleifera</span></em><span> can also be found in Central America, Africa, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia. Since </span><em><span>Moringa oleifera</span></em><span> cannot tolerate cold climates, it best thrives in these tropical and subtropical areas. Although the plant can survive in a variety of soil conditions, it prefers slightly acidic sandy or loamy soil. If over-watered, the roots of the plant easily rot, therefore the soil must be well-drained. The plant is commonly harvested for its leaves and fruits.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><div id="viewer-e05lv"><div><figure><div><div id="e05lv"><wow-image id="1-cardiovascular-health-ff7ab"></span><span><span>1. Cardiovascular health</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-b33be"><span><span>The diuretic properties, combined with lipid and blood pressure lowering elements, allow this plant to be used in treating cardiovascular diseases .<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a></sup> The main constituents used to treat cardiovascular disorders are found in the fluid of the plant’s leaves. The fluid was found to consist of nitrile, </span><strong><span>mustard oil glycosides</span></strong><span>, and thiocarbamate glycosides .<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a></sup> These acetylated glycosides that have an effect on cardiovascular disease are uncommonly found in the environment. </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-f6n1i"><span id="2-lowers-cholesterol-f6n1i"></span><span><span>2. Lowers cholesterol</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-dotd4"><span><span>The fruit has been shown to also lower cholesterol, triglycerides, and phospholipids. In one study, hypercholesteremic rabbits fed the moringa fruit had reduced liver, heart, and aorta lipid profiles and elevated fecal cholesterol .<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a></sup></span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-fojut"><span id="3-antioxidants-and-nutrients-fojut"></span><span><span>3. Antioxidants and nutrients</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-c22bt"><span><span>Moringa is a rich source of calcium, vitamin A, B vitamins, vitamin C, iron, and vitamin E, in addition to </span><a href="https://miracletrees.org/moringa-doc/moringa_the_miracle_tree.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>micronutrients</span></u></span></a><span> such as selenium, potassium, and zinc. Additionally, Moringa contains essential fatty acids, particularly </span><strong><span>oleic acid, </span></strong><span>a monounsaturated fat like olive oil. Its zeatin improves bioavailability of various nutrients, enhancing absorption. Its quercetin has antioxidant properties, stabilizing free radicals [</span><a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/B9780128021477000322" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>3</span></u></span></a><span>].</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><div id="viewer-d2jvv"><div><figure><div><div id="d2jvv"><wow-image id="fa29ad_1e55b1147bb84ba5a5ec568a0e00554f~mv2.jpg"><img alt="moringa leaves benefits and nutritional components" loading="lazy" src="/images/moringa-tree-10-benefits/02_fa29ad-1e55b1147bb84ba5a5ec568a0e00554f-.jpg"/></wow-image></div></div></figure></div></div></div><div type="image"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-8ccg3"><span id="4-neuroprotection-8ccg3"></span><span><span>4. Neuroprotection</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-dhds9"><span><span>Zeatin concentrations have been found to be thousands of times higher in moringa than in most other plants studied so far. Zeatin administered to mice can effectively protect them against </span><a href="https://miracletrees.org/moringa-doc/moringa_the_miracle_tree.pdf" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>neurodegeneration</span></u></span></a><span> and amyloid- and chemically-induced memory loss. It's also purported to be a nootropic that enhances memory by modifying </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3122981/#ref30" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>monoamine</span></u></span></a><span> neurotransmitter activity [</span><a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21716802/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>4</span></u></span></a><span>]. </span><strong><span>Isothiocyanate</span></strong><span> from moringa seeds also protected human neuronal cells against oxidative stress-induced cytotoxicity and </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29723199" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>apoptosis</span></u></span></a><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/29723199" rel="noopener" target="_blank"></a><span>[</span><a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29723199/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span><u><span>5</span></u></span></a><span>].</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-c650h"><span id="5-antimicrobial-and-antiviral-c650h"></span><span><span>5. Antimicrobial and antiviral</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-csrmn"><span><span>The roots of </span><em><span>Moringa oleifera</span></em><span> have both anti-bacterial and antifungal properties. The anti-microbial agent found in the roots is </span><strong><span>pterygospermin </span></strong><span>.<sup><a href="#ref-1">1</a></sup> This compound has antibacterial and fungicidal properties. 4-a-L-rhamnosyloxy benzyl isothiocyanate is another compound found in the roots that demonstrates antimicrobial and fungicidal properties .<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a></sup> It has shown to have substantial inhibitory effects against </span><strong><span>Epstein-Barr virus. </span></strong></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-enmmr"><span id="6-anti-cancer-and-antitumor-enmmr"></span><span><span>6. Anti-cancer and antitumor</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-f40o4"><span><span>A crude extract of </span><em><span>Moringa oleifera</span></em><span> leaves possesses a high concentration of different bioactive phytoconstituents such as </span><strong><span>beta-sitosterol, </span></strong><span>which can reduce the growth of certain cancer cells, such as prostate and colon cancer cells .<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a></sup> This compound has also had antitumor properties when paired with 4-(a-L-rhamnosyloxy) benzyl carbamate .<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a></sup> </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-7k7a0"><span id="7-anti-diabetic-7k7a0"></span><span><span>7. However, caution should be taken, because it can interact with prescription diabetes medications.</span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><div id="viewer-6d7vu"><div><figure><div><div id="6d7vu"><wow-image id="fa29ad_e5c8aed238bb404c8ada9d896a08dcbe~mv2_d_4032_3024_s_4_2.jpg"><img alt="" loading="lazy" src="/images/moringa-tree-10-benefits/05_fa29ad-e5c8aed238bb404c8ada9d896a08dcbe-.jpg"/></wow-image></div></div></figure></div></div></div><div type="image"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-4jago"><span id="8-protects-against-ulcers-4jago"></span><span><span>8. Protects liver</span></span></h2></div><div type="heading"></div><div><p id="viewer-6lrlg"><span><span>The plant has also shown to have hepatoprotective effects, which is believed to be a result of the presence of quercetin distributed throughout the plant .<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a></sup> </span></span></p></div><div type="paragraph"></div><div><h2 id="viewer-4m67o"><span id="10-water-purification-and-environmental-protection-4m67o"></span><span><span>10.
